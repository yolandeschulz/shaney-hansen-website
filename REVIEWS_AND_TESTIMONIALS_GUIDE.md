# Reviews & Testimonials Guide — for Shaney

This guide explains how reviews and testimonials work on the website, how to collect them, and the rules that keep them honest and privacy-safe.

## 1. Why Google reviews matter

When someone finds you online, reviews are usually the first thing they check. Real reviews from real clients build trust faster than anything you can write about yourself. They also help your Google Business Profile appear in local searches like "photographer Lichtenburg", and they matter even more once you start running Google Ads, because people compare advertisers.

## 2. How to ask clients for a review

The best moment is shortly after you deliver a gallery, while the excitement is fresh. A simple, personal message works well, for example: "It was so special photographing your family. If you enjoyed the experience, would you mind leaving a short Google review? It really helps a small business like mine — here's the link: [your review request link]". Ask after every successful shoot or project. Not everyone will respond, and that's fine — steady, honest reviews over time are worth more than a sudden burst.

## 3. Where to add the Google Review request link

Once you have a Google Business Profile, Google gives you a short "ask for reviews" link. Add it in Pages CMS under **Site Settings → Google Review request link**. The "Leave a Google Review" button then appears automatically on the website. Also use this link in WhatsApp messages after shoots, in your email signature, and on your Facebook/Instagram pages.

## 4. How to add testimonials in Pages CMS

Log in at app.pagescms.org, open **Testimonials**, and add an item. Fill in the client's real words (quote), their name, the source, the service type, and the date if you know it. Then tick **Client permission approved?** (only after asking them) and **Show on website?**. The testimonial appears on the site after the change is saved and the site rebuilds. Tick **Featured?** for the few best ones you want on the homepage.

## 5. How to label Google reviews properly

If you copy a review from Google onto the website, set the **Source** field to "Google Review" — the site displays it as "— [Name], Google Review" so visitors know where it came from. If you can, paste the link to the original review into the **Link to the original review** field.

## 6. Why not to create fake testimonials

Fake reviews are dishonest, illegal in many places (misleading advertising), and if discovered they destroy exactly the trust you are trying to build. They can also get a Google Business Profile suspended. The website is deliberately built so that empty placeholder testimonials never show — it is always better to show nothing than to show something invented.

## 7. Why not to invent star ratings

The same applies to star ratings. Only fill in the star rating field if the client actually gave that rating (for example in their Google review). The website never shows an overall rating, a review count or "5-star photographer" claims, and no review star markup is sent to Google from the website itself — that must never be added without confirming it is compliant.

## 8. How to choose testimonials for the homepage

The homepage shows only testimonials marked **Featured**. Choose two to four that are specific and warm ("Shaney made my nervous toddler laugh within minutes") rather than generic ("Great photographer!"), and that cover the services you most want to sell — for example one wedding, one family, one business/social media client.

## 9. How to use testimonials on service pages

Each service page automatically shows testimonials whose **Service type** matches that page: photography testimonials on the Photography page, social media testimonials on the Social Media page, wedding/travel/lifestyle ones on the Destination page, and styled/studio/cake-smash ones on the Creative Direction page. So always set the Service type field — that is what routes a testimonial to the right page.

## 10. How to update the Google Reviews button link

In Pages CMS, open **Site Settings** and paste the public link to your Google reviews into **Google Reviews link**. The "Read Google Reviews" buttons then appear on the homepage, contact page and footer automatically. While the field still contains a [placeholder], the buttons stay hidden — nothing breaks.

## 11. How to use a Google Review QR code

Google Business Profile can generate a QR code for your review link (or you can create one from the review request link with any QR generator). Upload the image in **Site Settings → Google Review QR code image**. Print it small on delivery notes, thank-you cards or studio signage so clients can scan and review on the spot. ## 12. How to keep testimonials privacy-safe

Always ask before publishing someone's words or name — that is what the **Client permission approved?** switch is for; the website will not show a testimonial without it. Use first names or "Name S." if the client prefers. Never publish testimonials that mention children's names, school names or private event details without explicit permission, and never attach a client photo to a testimonial unless they agreed to that specifically.
